<p><p>Portable Emissions Measurement Systems (PEMS) are devices used to measure and analyze the emissions from vehicles during real-world driving conditions, as opposed to laboratory tests. There are two types of PEMS market: Real-Driving Emissions (RDE) PEMS and Integrated PEMS (iPEMS). RDE PEMS focus on measuring exhaust emissions from vehicles, including pollutants such as nitrogen oxides and particulate matter. iPEMS, on the other hand, not only measure emissions but also integrate additional features like GPS, data logging, and remote diagnostics to provide a comprehensive analysis of a vehicle's performance and emissions.</p></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Elastomers are materials that have rubber-like elasticity and are used in various applications such as pharmaceuticals. There are two main types of elastomers in the pharmaceutical market: thermoset elastomers and thermoplastic elastomers. </p><p>Thermoset elastomers are cross-linked polymers that cannot be melted or reshaped once cured. They offer excellent chemical resistance and high-temperature stability. These properties make them suitable for applications that require long-term durability, such as in drug delivery devices.</p><p>On the other hand, thermoplastic elastomers are materials that can be melted and reshaped multiple times without losing their elasticity. They have a rubber-like nature and are often used in flexible packaging, seals, and closures for pharmaceutical products due to their ease of processing and cost-effectiveness.</p><p>Overall, both thermoset and thermoplastic elastomers play essential roles in the pharmaceutical industry by providing flexibility, durability, and stability in various applications.</p></p>

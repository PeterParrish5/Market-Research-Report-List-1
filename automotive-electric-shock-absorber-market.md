The increasing demand for better ride comfort and improved vehicle handling, along with the growing emphasis on vehicle safety, is driving the adoption of electric shock absorbers in the automotive industry. Electric shock absorbers offer various benefits such as improved stability, enhanced road-holding capability, reduced body roll, and reduced vibration, resulting in a smoother and more comfortable ride.</p><p>Additionally, the rising vehicle sales and increasing disposable income in emerging economies are further fueling the demand for automotive electric shock absorbers. <p><p>The automotive electric shock absorber market consists of three main types: conventional telescopic shock absorbers, strut type shock absorbers, and spring seat shock absorbers. Conventional telescopic shock absorbers are the most commonly used type and provide a smooth ride by absorbing shock from bumps and uneven roads. Strut type shock absorbers are integrated with the suspension system and enhance stability and handling. Spring seat shock absorbers combine both the features of a shock absorber and a spring, providing improved comfort and control over rough terrains.</p></p>

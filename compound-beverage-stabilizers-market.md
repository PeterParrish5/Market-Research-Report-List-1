The market includes different types such as Gum Arabic, Carboxymethyl Cellulose, Xanthan Gum, Carrageenan, and others. Gum Arabic is a natural stabilizer derived from the Acacia tree, while Carboxymethyl Cellulose is a synthetic stabilizer. Xanthan Gum, a microbial polysaccharide, provides viscosity and stability. Carrageenan, extracted from seaweed, is used as a gelling and thickening agent. Other stabilizers can include pectin, agar, and locust bean gum, which contribute to texture and stability in beverages.</p></p>

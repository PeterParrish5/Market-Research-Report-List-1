<p><p>Waterborne wood preservatives are chemicals that are used to protect wood from rot, decay, and insect damage. There are several types of waterborne wood preservatives available in the market. ACQ (alkaline copper quat) is a commonly used preservative that contains copper and quaternary ammonium compounds. Borates are another type, consisting of boron compounds that protect against fungal decay and insect attack. CCA (chromated copper arsenate) is a traditional preservative comprising copper, chromium, and arsenic. Other market alternatives include copper azole and copper naphthenate, which provide protection against decay fungi and termites.</p></p>
